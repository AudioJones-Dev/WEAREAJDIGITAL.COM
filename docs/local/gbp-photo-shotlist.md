# GBP Visual Asset System (Photo & Video)

## 1. PHOTO STRATEGY

### Why Photos Matter on GBP
Google Business Profiles with 100+ high-quality images see massive increases in calls, website clicks, and map direction requests compared to average profiles. For AJ Digital, photos are the primary mechanism to combat "agency vaporware"—proving that you have real equipment, real clients, and real expertise. 

### What Types Improve Trust & Local Relevance
- **Authenticity over Stock:** Never use stock photography. Google's Vision AI can detect stock images, and users immediately recognize them as fake.
- **High-End Context:** Your photos must sell "premium." Clean studios, sharp lighting, proper microphones (e.g., Shure SM7B), and organized workspaces.
- **Local Context:** Showing a recognizable South Florida skyline outside an office window, or geo-tagging the EXIF data of the photos to Miami-Dade locations, subtly reinforces geographic relevance.

## 2. SHOT LIST BY CATEGORY

Use this checklist to generate your visual asset library for deployment onto the GBP.

### Team / Founder Assets
- [ ] Professional headshot/portrait of the founder in a modern office or studio setting.
- [ ] Action shot of the founder speaking enthusiastically on a strategy call.
- [ ] Over-the-shoulder shot of the founder working on a complex digital workflow (Make/n8n/Descript) on dual monitors.

### Studio & Equipment (The "Podcast Production" Proof)
- [ ] Close-up depth-of-field shot of a premium microphone.
- [ ] Wide shot of a multi-camera podcast setup (lights, chairs, cameras).
- [ ] Close-up of the audio interface / mixing board / production equipment.
- [ ] Behind-the-scenes shot of a video editing timeline highlighting the "Post-Production" capability.

### On-Site / Client Environment
- [ ] Mobile production gear packed in premium Pelican cases (signals readiness to travel).
- [ ] Setting up lights/mics in a high-end corporate boardroom.
- [ ] The founder sitting down with a client across a table or on a podcast set.
- [ ] A satisfied client mid-laugh/smile during an interview.

### Branded Visuals & Results (The "Content Systems & AI" Proof)
- [ ] High-resolution export of the AJ Digital Logo.
- [ ] Clean infographic or screenshot showing an AI automated workflow pipeline.
- [ ] Graphic output showing a before/after metric (e.g., "1 Recording → 30 Assets").
- [ ] Carousel/Graphic showing a thumbnail of a podcast alongside its repurposed formats (a Tweet, a Reel, a Blog screenshot).

## 3. PHOTO CADENCE

Consistency is key. Google prefers profiles that add photos regularly over time rather than dumping 50 photos once and never touching the profile again.

### The Launch Batch (Day 1)
- Upload **15 core photos** spanning all categories above to establish the baseline visual identity.
- Set the high-res Logo.
- Set the Cover Photo (recommend the "Wide shot of a multi-camera podcast setup").

### Weekly Cadence
- Upload **1 to 2 new photos per week**.
- Tie this into the Weekly GBP Posting schedule. When you write a post, upload the image directly to the "Photos" tab as well.

### Monthly Refresh
- Check which photos are receiving the most views. Keep generating similar content.
- Delete/flag any user-generated photos that are irrelevant, blurry, or damaging to the premium brand standard.

## 4. FILE NAMING & ORGANIZATION STANDARD

Before uploading any image to Google, you must rename the raw file. Image filenames act as micro-ranking signals for Google's crawlers.

**Standard Convention:**
`[primary-keyword]-[location]-[descriptor].jpg`

**Good Examples:**
- `podcast-production-miami-studio-setup.jpg`
- `ai-consulting-fort-lauderdale-strategy-session.jpg`
- `content-repurposing-systems-workflow-dashboard.jpg`
- `aj-digital-founder-audio-engineer-miami.jpg`

**Bad Examples:**
- `IMG_8472.jpg`
- `Untitled_design(1).png`
- `headshot_final_v2.jpg`

*Note: Ensure images are compressed (under 2MB, ideally JPEGs or WebP) to ensure fast loading on mobile devices while retaining high visual fidelity.*
