# GBP Review Generation & Response System

## 1. REVIEW ACQUISITION STRATEGY

Google Business Profile reviews are the primary social proof mechanism determining Map Pack ranking and high-ticket conversion rates. Generating a consistent velocity of 5-star reviews requires a programmatic approach, not hoping the client remembers to do it.

### Who to ask
- Clients who have just completed a successful strategy/audit session.
- Clients who have just launched the first episode of their podcast.
- Clients who report a major "win" (e.g., getting a lead from repurposed content, saving 10 hours a week from an AI workflow).

### When to ask (The "Milestone" Method)
Do not wait until the very end of an engagement. Ask at the height of emotional satisfaction:
1. **Milestone 1:** Immediately following a high-value Consulting/Strategy call.
2. **Milestone 2:** The day their automated AI pipeline goes live and works flawlessly.
3. **Milestone 3:** The day their flagship podcast episode drops on Spotify/Apple.

### How to ask
- Always ask precisely and individually. 
- Provide the exact direct review link (accessible from your GBP dashboard).
- Suggest specific topics for them to mention to make it easier for them to write (and to seed keywords naturally).

### How often to ask
- Limit outbox requests to 2-3 per week to ensure a natural, organic velocity of incoming reviews. A massive spike of 20 reviews in one day can trigger Google's spam filters.

## 2. REVIEW THEMES TO ENCOURAGE NATURALLY

When asking for a review, gently prompt the client to mention specific aspects of your service. This naturally injects keywords into your reviews, which Google parses for ranking relevance.

**Prompting Themes:**
- **The Service:** "Podcast production," "AI consulting," "Content systems."
- **The Location:** "In Miami," "came to our Fort Lauderdale office."
- **The Outcome:** "Automated my workflow," "saved the team hours," "we got 30 assets from one recording."
- **The Qualitative Traits:** "Professionalism," "highly organized," "systems-focused."

## 4. REVIEW RESPONSE FRAMEWORK

Google expects active businesses to respond to all reviews. Furthermore, your responses are public content that Google indexes. 

### Responding to 5-Star Reviews
**Structure:** Thank them by name → Acknowledge the specific work/service → Reinforce the local intent or system outcome.
**Example:** *"Thanks so much, David! It was fantastic coming out to your office in Brickell to set up the on-site podcast production. Seeing how quickly your team adapted to the new content system has been great. Looking forward to the next recording session!"*

### Responding to Neutral Reviews (3-4 Stars)
**Structure:** Thank them for taking the time → Acknowledge the positive → Professionally address any friction organically.
**Example:** *"Thank you for the feedback, Sarah! We're thrilled that the AI workflow automation is saving your operations team time every week. We're always looking to refine our onboarding process for our Miami clients, and we appreciate your partnership as we continue to scale the system together."*

### Responding to Negative Reviews (1-2 Stars)
**Structure:** De-escalate → Move the conversation offline immediately. **Never argue publicly.**
**Example:** *"Hi [Name], thank you for bringing this to our attention. We pride ourselves on building premium, friction-free content systems for our partners, and we're sorry to hear this didn't meet our usual standards. Please reach out to me directly at [Email/Phone] so I can personally investigate and resolve this for you."* 

*(Note: If a negative review is fake/spam, report it to Google immediately via the GBP dashboard).*
